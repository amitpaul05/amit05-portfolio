In one of our ongoing projects, Govara, we needed a centralized audit logging feature to keep track of important user actions.

I spent most of the day building the logging foundation. Created the database model, a reusable logging service, and integrated CloudWatch for production monitoring. The interesting part was authentication logging. After some investigation, I found that SimpleJWT bypasses Django's built-in login signal, which meant my initial approach wouldn't work.

Once I understood the flow, I moved the logging directly into the token authentication process. By the end of the day, login, logout, and failed login attempts were being captured with the relevant user and request information.
